§ 01

Executive summary

NGW monetizes a single, well-defined behavior: a working photographer points the camera at a reference image, gets back a decoded lighting setup they can actually rebuild. The monetization model converts that moment of resolved ambiguity into recurring revenue through a tiered subscription with an adaptive paywall.

Core thesis

Photographers don't pay for "AI analysis." They pay to stop guessing on lighting they can already see but can't yet reproduce.

The free tier proves the engine on three images. The paid tiers turn that proof into a daily working tool — Shoot Mode, kit matching, Shot Match, and reproducible setups.

Why this model works

High intrinsic-value moments (a successful reconstruction) anchor higher price points. Low-value moments anchor entry pricing. The adaptive pricing engine routes each user to the right offer at the right moment.

Result: a single product surface, three durable tiers, and a paywall that respects intent rather than fighting it.

The three revenue surfaces
1 · Subscription

Monthly & yearly Pro / Studio plans. The base recurring revenue line.

2 · Adaptive paywall

Price ladder $39 / 49 / 59 / 79 routed by intent state, with session-level anti-discount guardrails.

3 · Studio & API

Team seats, batch processing, and metered REST access (100 req/hr) — the upgrade path beyond the working solo photographer.

NGW·Revenue § 02Positioning & Who Pays
§ 02

Positioning & who pays

NGW is not a general "photo AI" tool. It is positioned as the lighting decode and reconstruction layer for working photographers — a category of one. Pricing reflects that positioning rather than chasing generic SaaS comps.

Primary buyer
Working solo photographer

Portrait, beauty, fashion, editorial. Bills clients. Owns or rents lighting kit. Lives at the $39–$59/mo SaaS threshold for tools that compress one shoot's worth of reshoots.

Secondary buyer
Small studio & production team

2–6 collaborators sharing references, kit, and reconstructed setups. Buys Studio for batch, white-label export, setup sharing, and REST API access.

Adjacent / non-buyer
Hobbyist & learner

Lives on Free tier. Three analyses + reference library. Not the revenue target — the credibility and word-of-mouth flywheel for the paying audience.

Why a working photographer pays
  • 01Decode time saved. A 20-minute "what light did they use?" exercise compressed into a single analysis with a reproducible diagram.
  • 02Kit-aware reconstruction. Shoot Mode matches the setup to gear the photographer actually owns. The answer is buildable today, not in theory.
  • 03Reference reproducibility. Setups save, export, and share. The platform becomes the photographer's external memory.
  • 04Credibility under ambiguity. The engine names what's unresolved. Photographers trust tools that admit uncertainty over tools that bluff.
  • 05Master Modes & Build From Scratch. The platform is also a teacher — a structured way to extend the photographer's own range.
  • 06Premium positioning. Priced like a working tool, not a consumer app. Aligned to the buyer's professional self-image.

Conversion funnel

The funnel below shows the stages each acquired visitor moves through. Percentages are Modeled targets for the base scenario, calibrated against the existing free-trial structure (14 days, no card). Real cohort behavior replaces these once instrumentation runs at volume.

Stage 01 Visitor 100 % Lands on marketing or reference library.
Stage 02 Signup · Free 35 % Creates account, runs first of 3 analyses.
Stage 03 Trial start 12 % Begins 14-day Pro trial, no card required.
Stage 04 Paid · Pro 5.1 % 78 % of paid mix · adaptive paywall.
Stage 05 Paid · Studio 1.4 % 22 % of paid mix · team / batch / API.
Funnel drop-off · per 25,000 monthly visitors (Base) Free flow Revenue

Funnel widths and absolute counts are proportional. Gold stages are revenue-bearing tiers. Studio count includes Pro seats that upgraded.

Where the funnel earns its keep

Stage 02 → 03. The trial-start rate is the most leveraged step. It's driven by the engine's ability to deliver a credible reconstruction within the free 3 analyses.

Stage 03 → 04. Trial-to-paid is governed by the adaptive paywall (see § 06). The engine routes by intent state, not by uniform pricing.

Stage 04 → 05. Pro-to-Studio is structural: when a photographer brings a collaborator or batch workflow, Studio is the natural upgrade.

What moves these numbers
  • + pattern accuracy — every 10 pp gain in pattern match lifts trial-to-paid ~3 pp Modeled per the revenue.py heuristic.
  • + time-to-first-aha — reducing time-to-credible-reconstruction within Free gates trial-start rate.
  • + Master Modes adoption — users who run any preset within trial convert materially higher.
  • + saved-setup recall — return-to-platform frequency in week 2 of trial is a leading indicator.

§ 07

Adaptive pricing

The paywall reads the user's value state — what just happened in the engine — and routes them to a price, message, and CTA tuned to that moment. Logic implemented in engine/paywall/adaptive_pricing.py.

Price ladder · live
$39Entry · low-friction
$49Intelligence boost
$59Success / high-intent
$79Experiment ceiling

Snap-to-ladder. The engine never invents a price between rungs — it composes the next rung from a base price + intent boost + experiment variant, then snaps. Anti-discount guardrails prevent oscillation within a session.

Adjustments
  • + $10 when intelligence score ≥ 0.80, then snap-to-ladder.
  • + $10 on experiment_variant = price_high, snap-to-ladder.
  • Yearly = 10 × monthly, ≈17 % effective discount.
  • Guardrail — never show a lower price than the session high. No oscillation, no race-to-bottom.
State → price routing State node Price rung
Why adaptive — and why bounded

A photographer who just got a clean reconstruction is in a different commercial state than one who hit ambiguity. A uniform $39 paywall undercharges the first and overcharges the second. The adaptive engine corrects that without resorting to dark-pattern urgency.

The ladder is short (4 rungs) and price moves are bounded by guardrails. This protects two things: (1) the user's trust — no whipsaw pricing, (2) the brand's premium position — no race to discount.

NGW·Revenue § 08Growth Levers & Risks
§ 08

Growth levers & risks

Three levers move ARR materially in the base scenario. Three risks would move it backwards. Each is named, sized, and tied to a concrete part of the system.

Levers

01
Pattern accuracy compounding
Per the revenue.py heuristic, a +10 pp pattern-match gain projects a ~3 pp trial-to-paid lift. Engine accuracy is the single most leveraged growth input — and it compounds with usage.
02
Studio mix expansion
Shifting Pro / Studio mix from 78 / 22 to 70 / 30 lifts blended ARPU by ~$1.60/mo without changing acquisition. Driven by team collaboration, batch, and REST API surface.
03
Yearly attach rate
Yearly at 10 × monthly is structurally cash-flow positive. Every 10 pp shift from monthly → yearly at signup pulls forward ~6 months of revenue per seat.
04
Word-of-mouth flywheel
Setup sharing (Studio) and the community reference library are the organic-acquisition surface. Sharing rate × visit-to-signup is the cheapest CAC line on the spreadsheet.

Risks

Engine credibility regression
A single high-profile misclassification, or a habit of bluffing under ambiguity, breaks the photographer-trust thesis. Mitigation: CLAUDE.md § III VL — VLM is hinting, not truth. Engine names what's unresolved.
COGS drift on VLM
If routing logic loosens, VLM per-seat cost can double. Mitigation: deterministic CV runs first; VLM bounded by intent + ambiguity gates (CLAUDE.md § VII).
Adaptive paywall calibration
A mis-calibrated state mapping prices the wrong moment. Mitigation: ladder is short, guardrails prevent oscillation, experiment variants are bounded.
Substitution by generic photo-AI
Lighting decode + kit-aware reconstruction is the moat. Mitigation: depth of reference library, taxonomy rigor, Master Modes specificity — not features a generic tool will copy quickly.
